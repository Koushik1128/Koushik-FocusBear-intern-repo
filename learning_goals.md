# Learning Goals

**What do you hope to learn from this internship?**

Mainly I want real world data analytics experience, something beyond coursework and personal projects. RMIT has given me the theory and tools, but there's a gap between working with clean datasets in an assignment and dealing with actual messy company data, real stakeholders, and decisions that matter. This internship is my chance to close that gap before I go looking for a Data Analyst role properly.

**What skills are you most excited to develop?**

I'm most excited to get better with the actual tools and workflows a working data analyst uses day to day, dashboards, reporting pipelines, the kind of technical setup that doesn't really show up until you're inside a real team. I came in with a background in Azure monitoring and observability from my time at Wipro, so I'm curious to see how much of that transfers versus how much is genuinely new here.

**What do you think you can contribute to the team?**

I bring a mix that's a bit unusual, an electrical engineering undergrad, a few years of professional experience on enterprise monitoring and KPI reporting, and now a Master's focused on data science with some RAG and multi agent system work in my current coursework. I think that combination means I can pick up new tools quickly and also bring a slightly different perspective than someone coming straight from a pure data science background.

**What is one area you feel less confident in that you'd like to improve?**

Technical and tool skills, specifically. I know the concepts well from study, but there's a real difference between knowing a concept and being fast and confident with the actual tooling in a live team setting. That's honestly the main thing I want this internship to fix.

**What does success in this internship look like for you?**

Success looks like finishing this with genuine, defensible real world experience I can talk about in interviews, not just a line on a resume. I'm aiming at Data Analyst or Senior Data Analyst roles afterwards, likely at a Big 4 firm or one of the larger Australian tech companies, so success here means walking away comfortable enough with the tools and workflows that I'm not starting from zero on day one of whatever comes next.
